Queen Conch Tracking Device

In order to track conch movement, my team of three other design students worked with researchers at Shedd aquarium to create a new snail tracker using an accelerometer sensor package, analyzing data with Arduino so that researchers could determine what areas would be best suited for designation as Marine Protected Areas (MPAs).

Northwestern Undergraduate Research Journal (NURJ) Projects

Accessibility in research can be fostered in more ways than one can count, as I've learned through leading the online sector of the NURJ. Seeing research manifest itself in areas where it was not labeled as such, I decided to create a team project investigating the creation of art through the framework of research methodology. Noting the general anxieties of first-time pursuers of research grants and wanting to strengthen my ability to present such information in a digestible way, I teamed up with NURJ Development Manager Kevin Bye to create a series of video interview clips interspersed with written segments to provide peer guidance. I learned how to write comedy sketches in the process! Channeling the aggravation fueled by the presentation of figures in neuroscience papers, I set my team upon the task of spending a quarter researching and featuring collaborations between artists, data scientists, and scientific researchers.
